Gyeongbokgung Palace built in 1395, is the largest and most iconic palace of the Joseon Dynasty in Seoul, South Korea. It served as the main royal residence and the seat of government for centuries. The palace features majestic architecture, beautiful gates, and spacious courtyards, symbolizing the authority and dignity of the monarchy. Gyeongbokgung also houses the National Palace Museum and the National Folk Museum, offering insights into Korean history and culture. Today, it is a popular tourist attraction and a site for the traditional changing of the guard ceremony.

Changdeokgung Palace is one of the Five Grand Palaces of Seoul, built during the Joseon Dynasty in the early 15th century. It is famous for its harmonious integration with the surrounding natural landscape, particularly its secret rear garden called Huwon. The palace served as the residence of many kings and was the center of state affairs for centuries. Its architecture combines traditional Korean design with practical layouts suited to nature and seasonal changes. Today, Changdeokgung is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular destination for visitors interested in history, culture, and scenic beauty.
