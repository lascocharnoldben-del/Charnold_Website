Seoraksan National Park

(Cultural Tourism)

Cultural tourism in South Korea allows visitors to experience the country’s rich traditions, history, and heritage through activities such as visiting historic palaces, traditional villages, festivals, and museums. Tourists can explore famous sites like Gyeongbokgung Palace and Bukchon Hanok Village while learning about Korean customs, arts, and cuisine.


Seoraksan National Park, located in Gangwon Province, is one of South Korea’s most famous and scenic national parks. It is renowned for its rugged granite peaks, lush forests, and diverse wildlife, including the rare Korean goral. The park offers a variety of hiking trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging climbs, with popular spots like Ulsanbawi Rock and Biryong Falls. In autumn, the mountains are especially famous for their vibrant foliage, attracting thousands of visitors each year. Seoraksan is also home to Buddhist temples, such as Sinheungsa, which blend cultural heritage with the natural beauty of the park.
