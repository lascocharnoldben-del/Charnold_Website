Religious tourism in South Korea involves visiting temples, shrines, and other sacred sites to explore the country’s spiritual and cultural heritage. Many travelers visit places like Jogyesa Temple to learn about Korean Buddhism and participate in traditional rituals. This type of tourism allows visitors to experience meditation, ceremonies, and historical architecture. Religious tourism also provides insight into Korea’s long-standing spiritual traditions. It is an important part of cultural tourism, attracting both domestic and international visitors.
