Seoraksan National Park, located in Gangwon Province, South Korea, is renowned for its rugged granite peaks, dense forests, and diverse wildlife. The park is famous for its hiking trails, ranging from easy walks to challenging climbs, including iconic spots like Ulsanbawi Rock and Biryong Falls. In autumn, the mountains attract thousands of visitors with their vibrant fall foliage. Seoraksan is also home to historic Buddhist temples, such as Sinheungsa, blending cultural heritage with natural beauty. Today, it is a popular destination for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, offering breathtaking landscapes year-round.
