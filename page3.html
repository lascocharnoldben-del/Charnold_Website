Heritage culture in South Korea reflects the country’s long history, traditions, and customs that have been preserved for generations. It can be seen in historic sites like Gyeongbokgung Palace, traditional villages such as Bukchon Hanok Village, and cultural practices including traditional clothing, food, and festivals.

Explore Bukchon Hanok Village, a traditional Korean village nestled in the heart of Seoul. The village preserves hundreds of beautifully maintained hanok — classic Korean-style houses — and sits between the iconic Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung Palaces. Visitors can wander through picturesque streets, admire traditional architecture, and enjoy cultural centers, tea houses, and craft shops. Bukchon offers a unique glimpse into Korea’s rich heritage and is a popular destination for experiencing authentic cultural activities and history.
